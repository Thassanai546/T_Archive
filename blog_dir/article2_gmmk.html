The GMMK Pro

A look into the award-winning mechanical gaming keyboard base with enthusiast components and unmatched build quality.

The Body

The GMMK Pro is a 75% keyboard with 87 keys. This was perfect as I still had dedicated function keys alongside arrow keys. I also really like the aluminum body with its anodized finish. This keyboard chassis alone was $169.99 X_X


Switches

With a name like Holy Panda, these switches had to be good. They absolutely were with an excellent snappy and tactile feel.

These switches were pretty costly. They were sold in batches of 35 switches for $42 each. I bought 3 batches totalling 105 rocks for some keyboard switches. Needless to say, I had high expectations for the final build.



The build

Hot-swap switches made this board nice and easy to put together. I got a coiled cable and double shot PBT key caps. I highly recommend getting double shot PBT key caps as they are generally great quality and last a long time. While I was now down around $300, I found that the build turned out great. It's an amazing feeling, and sounding keyboard. For now, I do not see myself spending this much on another keyboard going forward. My poor poor wallet :(
